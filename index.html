Sigh List

What in your life or work makes you sigh?

Credit Artie Kuhn at Miami University here.

Start collecting friction

  • The task you keep postponing because it is tedious
  • The workflow that breaks every time the format changes
  • The thing only one person knows how to do
Frame the sigh list as a practical inventory of recurring friction, not a vague brainstorming exercise.

What types of problems?

Campus use cases are everywhere once you start looking for repeated friction.

  • exam questions uploaded into Canvas
  • chemical diagrams
  • learning objects
  • interlinear Bible
These are examples of tool-worthy problems, not just chat prompts.

Pattern recognition

If the same annoyance happens again and again, it may not be a personal productivity problem. It may be a missing tool.

Name the shift explicitly: repeated friction is a signal that a tool may be worth building.
